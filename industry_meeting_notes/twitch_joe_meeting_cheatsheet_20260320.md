# Twitch Joe Meeting Cheat Sheet

## Role

- [Data Scientist, Twitch](https://job-boards.greenhouse.io/twitch/jobs/8401220002)
- San Francisco, CA
- Central analytics and finance
- Reports to the VP of Finance, Analytics, and Business Operations

## My Read On The Role

- This looks like a strong business-facing data science role.
- The work appears centered on viewer behavior, monetization, dashboards, SQL-heavy analysis, and narrative support for decision-making.
- This does not look like an experimentation-first or causal-inference-first role.
- It looks closer to business analytics, product analytics, and finance/monetization support.

## Fit

- Overall fit: good, around 4/5.
- Strong overlap:
  - SQL-heavy analysis
  - KPI development and dashboarding
  - growth, retention, and MAU analysis
  - business-facing communication
  - turning ambiguous questions into structured analysis
- Main gaps:
  - less direct finance, ad revenue, and commerce experience
  - no direct gaming background
  - resume can read more research-heavy than this role likely wants

## My Best Angles

- Pandora is highly relevant for this role.
- I have experience designing and measuring lifecycle campaigns, tracking KPIs, building dashboards, and supporting recurring decision workflows.
- I can speak credibly about metrics, retention, growth, and business impact.
- My PhD and Spotify work add rigor, but I should frame them in terms of business questions and product outcomes, not academic contribution.

## What To Emphasize

- strong SQL and dashboarding comfort
- KPI design and recurring measurement
- forecasting and performance tracking
- experience supporting business and product decisions with data
- ability to communicate results clearly to non-technical stakeholders

## What To Downplay

- very academic framing
- overly research-oriented language
- deep AI or causal-inference framing unless he asks
- creator-economics language unless I tie it back to business outcomes

## Notes From Joe

- There may still be layoffs or at least continued organizational uncertainty.
- Data Engineering is still present, and Data Science is also still present, but the broader environment feels unsettled.
- Amazon broadly announced another wave of layoffs.
- It feels like a period where the longer-term vision and impact are not fully figured out yet.
- The role sounds more reporting-heavy than I initially thought.
- It may involve financial reporting for Amazon, monthly pulse checks, and understanding what else the organization is working on.
- This role sits in a more embedded functional group.
- There is a separate group focused on safety and moderation.
- The rest of Data Science and Data Engineering is centralized under monetization.
- Joe sits in a central Data Engineering team that supports the content data science team.
- That work includes a variety of different things: activity, user growth story, and instrumentation.
- There is a commerce analytics group covering ads, ad platform, and payments pipelines.
- That group has more direct money experimentation.
- There is also a community data science group focused on product-surface experimentation, browse and recommendation systems, user flow, and mobile experience.
- Those are the core three.
- Less focus on content, more on instrumentation.
- Open question: how easy is it to move internally across these groups once you are at Twitch?
