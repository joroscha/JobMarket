# Mock Questions

## Causal inference

1. How would you estimate the incremental impact of a marketing campaign on paid subscriptions if randomization is not possible?
2. What assumptions are required for propensity score matching to identify a causal effect?
3. What is the difference between confounding and collider bias?
4. When would you use difference-in-differences instead of matching?
5. How would you explain positivity and ignorability to a product manager?
6. What variables would you adjust for in a campaign effectiveness study, and which would you avoid adjusting for?

## Experimentation

1. Design an experiment to measure the lift from a new Amazon Music promotional surface.
2. What should the randomization unit be for a campaign shown across multiple sessions?
3. How would you choose the primary metric and guardrails?
4. What would you do if the experiment shows no statistically significant effect?
5. How would you detect and respond to sample ratio mismatch?
6. How would you handle spillovers if exposed users can influence unexposed users?

## Bayesian

1. Explain Bayes theorem in plain English.
2. What is the difference between a credible interval and a confidence interval?
3. How would you use a Beta-Binomial model for subscription conversion?
4. When is Bayesian A/B testing more useful than a standard frequentist test?
5. Why does hierarchical Bayes help with sparse geo-level data?
6. How would you make a launch decision from a posterior distribution instead of a p-value?

## Mixed case prompts

1. Amazon Music launches a new artist promotion campaign in selected geos. How would you measure incrementality?
2. A marketing stakeholder says attribution reports prove the campaign worked. How do you respond?
3. You cannot run a clean user-level RCT because the campaign is bought at the market level. What design do you use?
4. The treatment effect is positive overall but negative for a high-value segment. How do you investigate?
5. A leader wants one number for ROI, but the identification strategy is imperfect. How do you present the result?
